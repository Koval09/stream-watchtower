🔑 Twitch Login & Cookie Fix

Embedded Twitch streams use security-isolated <iframe>s. Modern browsers block third-party cookies by default, which prevents the embedded player from recognizing your Twitch account (even if you are logged in on twitch.tv in another tab).

This triggers Twitch's multi-stream limits and displays the permanent "Commercial break in progress" screen.

How to fix this:

  1. Add a Cookie Exception in Chrome Settings (Recommended):
    • Copy this address: chrome://settings/cookies and open it in a new browser tab.
    • Scroll down to the "Sites allowed to use third-party cookies" (Разрешено использовать сторонние файлы cookie) section.
    • Click the "Add" (Добавить) button.
    • Enter exactly: [*.]twitch.tv and click Add.
    • Go back to StreamWatchtower and refresh the page!
  2. Alternative: Use the link in the popover:
    • Click the circular info icon ⓘ next to localhost:8000.
    • Select "Cookies and site data" (Файлы cookie и данные сайтов).
    • Click the underlined "Settings" (Настройки) link in the text description.
    • This will take you to Chrome settings where you can add the [*.]twitch.tv exception as described in step 1.
  3. Log In inside the Embed:
    • Open any stream in Focus Mode, click the "Log In / Войти" button inside the Chat sidebar, and complete the Twitch login.
💡 Pro-Tip: Once third-party cookies are allowed, Twitch embeds will securely read your main twitch.tv session. If you have Twitch Turbo or are subscribed to the channel, all ads and "Commercial Break" warnings will vanish!
